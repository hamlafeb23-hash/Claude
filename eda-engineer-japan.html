What does a EDA Engineer do?

EDA (Electronic Design Automation) Engineers develop, deploy, and optimize the software tools and flows that the rest of the semiconductor industry relies on to design chips. The role sits at the intersection of software engineering and chip design methodology — EDA engineers may write new EDA tool features, build and maintain internal design flows, automate repetitive design tasks, or develop custom analyses for specific design challenges.

In practice, EDA engineers work with languages like Tcl, Python, Perl, and C++ alongside commercial EDA platforms from Synopsys, Cadence, and Siemens EDA. They develop scripts that run synthesis, place-and-route, simulation, and sign-off flows at scale, ensuring consistency and reproducibility across large design teams. At EDA companies themselves, the role shifts toward product development, algorithm research, and customer support. At fabless chip companies, EDA engineers are increasingly called "CAD engineers" or "design methodology engineers." The role is critical infrastructure for the entire industry.
