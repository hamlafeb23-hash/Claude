What does a Embedded Systems Engineer do?

Embedded Systems Engineers design, develop, and validate software and hardware systems where computing resources are constrained and real-time performance is critical. In the semiconductor context, this means developing firmware for chips, FPGA prototyping platforms, evaluation boards, and reference designs — as well as writing drivers and middleware that customers use to integrate semiconductors into their end products.

The role typically involves RTOS programming (FreeRTOS, Zephyr, ThreadX), hardware bring-up, driver development (SPI, I2C, UART, PCIe), and performance profiling. Embedded engineers at semiconductor companies also work on SDK development and customer support, helping OEMs and ODMs integrate chips into devices ranging from automotive ECUs to consumer wearables. Proficiency in C, assembly, and real-time debugging is essential. With the proliferation of connected devices and automotive electronics, embedded systems engineers are increasingly expected to understand security, over-the-air update mechanisms, and functional safety (ISO 26262, IEC 61508).

Key Skills Required

Embedded C / C++ programming RTOS (FreeRTOS, Zephyr, VxWorks) ARM Cortex-M / Cortex-A architecture Peripheral drivers (SPI, I2C, UART, CAN) Hardware debugging (JTAG, oscilloscope) Linux BSP and device driver development Automotive standards (AUTOSAR) a plus
